Robert Llewellyn Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Career

Robert Llewellyn was born in Northampton, England. His entry into show business began as a hobby, organizing amateur cabaret evenings in London.

This led to the formation of an alternative comedy theatre group called The Joeys, with Llewellyn eventually quitting his job as a shoemaker to perform professionally.

The group explored sexual politics and toured Britain and France in the early 1980s. After the group disbanded in 1985, Llewellyn co-wrote and starred in The Corner House, a sitcom about a café run by gay men.

Red Dwarf

His association with Red Dwarf began after his performance in a one-man comedy at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe caught the attention of the show’s producer, leading to his role as Kryten.

He joined the cast in 1989 and contributed to the series by co-writing an episode and playing additional characters. Llewellyn’s portrayal of Kryten required extensive makeup, and he was the only British cast member initially involved in a proposed American version of the show.

Other Television Work

Beyond Red Dwarf, Llewellyn has presented several UK television programs, including Scrapheap Challenge and How Do They Do It? on the Discovery Channel.
