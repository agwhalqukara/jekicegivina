What is Jasmine Camacho-Quinn's net worth, salary and brand endorsements?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, August 13, 2024

Puerto Rico’s Jasmine Camacho-Quinn is the defending Olympic champion in the women’s 100m hurdles after setting the Olympic record in the semifinals. Her finals time of 12.37 was sufficient to place her on top of the podium and become the Olympic gold medalist. That was only slightly slower than her semifinals performance of 12.26, which broke the previous Games record set by Australia’s Sally Pearson in 2012. She set the record of 12.35 seconds when she won gold in London nearly a decade ago. Quinn had the fastest time ever in the 100-meter hurdles in 2022 as well as winning 12 out of 13 games going into the Olympics in 2021.

The two-time Olympian was born in Charleston, South Carolina, where both of her parents were track and field athletes at Baptist College, which is now Charleston Southern University. She comes from a family with a history in athletics. Her father, James Quinn, also competed in hurdles, while her mother, Mara Milagros Camacho, was a sprinter and long jumper. Camacho-mother Quinn’s was born in Puerto Rico, hence she is entitled to represent the island. Her brother, Robert Quinn, is an American football defensive end with the National Football League’s Philadelphia Eagles (NFL).

In the 2017–18 season, Camacho-Quinn made her professional debut and committed to working with Nike on a long-term basis. Since that time, Nike has been the main sponsor of the gold medalist from the Tokyo Olympics. She even appeared in a commercial for a sports shoe company alongside American Paralympic volleyball player Nicky Nieves that celebrated the Puerto Rican Parade in New York by showcasing Puerto Rican culture through Puerto Ricans born in the country. The Nike NYC commercial, ‘Family,’ which debuted on social media, featured a debate amongst the island’s top athletes, chefs, and musicians.
