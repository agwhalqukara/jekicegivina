I Muvrini Full Overview and Wiki

The Roots of Polyphony: Corsica Calls

I Muvrini, a name synonymous with Corsican folk music, resonates through the mountains of Corsica much like the mouflon, the wild sheep from which they take their name.

Founded in the 1980s by brothers Jean-François and Alain Bernardini, the group has flourished under the influence of their father, Ghjuliu Bernardini, a revered poet and singer who introduced them to the depths of traditional Corsican music.

Promoting Corsican Culture

Dedicated to the promotion of the Corsican language and culture, they not only preserve but also revitalizes traditional Corsican sounds.

The brothers have seamlessly woven the threads of their heritage with modern influences, creating a tapestry rich in polyphonic singing and musical poetry.

Their efforts have catapulted Corsican music onto the world stage, making a compelling case for the island’s cultural richness.

A Discography of Cultural Wealth

From their first album, …Ti ringrazianu, dedicated to their late father in 1979, to the latest release Piu forti in 2022, their discography is both extensive and impressive.

Notable albums like Umani and Curagiu showcase their evolution from traditional roots to a more contemporary sound, integrating various musical influences while staying true to their origins.

Collaborations and Awards

Their collaborative works, notably with Sting on Terre d’Oru (Fields of Gold), highlight their versatility and ability to cross cultural boundaries. Such partnerships have not only enriched their musical repertoire but also broadened their audience.

The recognition they have received is evident in multiple awards, including the prestigious Victoires de la Musique for their albums Umani and I Muvrini à Bercy, solidifying their status in the world music scene.

FAQs

What is I Muvrini?

This is a Corsican folk music group known for singing traditional Corsican songs in their native Corsican language. The group promotes Corsican language and culture through their music.

Who are the founding members of I Muvrini?

The group was founded by brothers Jean-François Bernardini and Alain Bernardini in the early 1980s. They were inspired by their father, Ghjuliu, a well-known poet and singer .

What does the name ‘I Muvrini’ mean?

This name translates to ‘The Mouflons’ in English, referring to a species of wild sheep found in the mountains of Corsica .

When was I Muvrini formed?

This group was formed in 1979 and their first album was released that same year .

What are some notable collaborations by this group?

Over the years, they have collaborated with various artists, including Sting with whom they recorded Terre d’Oru (Fields of Gold) in 2000 .

What genres does their music cover?

Their music covers genres like folk and worldbeat, deeply rooted in European and international folk traditions.

Can you list some albums by Muvrini?

Some notable albums include Curagiu (1995), Leia (1998), Umani (2002), Alma (2005), and Piu forti (2022) .

What awards have they won?

The group has won the Victoires de la Musique award twice; once in 1997 for their album I Muvrini à Bercy and again in 2003 for Umani .

Where are the Bernardini brothers from?

Both brothers were born in Tagliu-Isulacciu, in the north of Corsica.

Have they performed internationally?

Yes, they have performed extensively internationally, helping to bring Corsican music to a global audience.
